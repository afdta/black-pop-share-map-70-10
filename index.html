Change in the black share of the population, 1970 to 2010

6,000+ cities

How to read this
(Sorry, legends are tedious to create and I don't want to burn time on that if we go in a different direction)

Line angle indicates change in black share:

  1. Vertical, pointed UP: 30+ percentage point increase in black share
  2. Horizontal/flat: No change in black share
  3. Vertical, pointed DOWN: 30+ percentage point decrease in black share
  4. E.g. +/-45 degree line is +/- 15 percentage point decrease, and so on

Line and dot color indicates gain / loss in black population share

Dot size corresponds with total population in 2010

Notes

This is very much a draft, though I think it is the best of my early sketches. It doesn't directly depict shifts in the white population, though that is represented implicitely (e.g. if the black share increases, the non-black share decreases, and vice versa).

I think this representation shows promise. The challenge is that there's a lot of information to convey. E.g. this map doesn't depict the absolute size (25 pct. pt. change in a small city is the same here, visually, as 25 pct. pt. change in a big city) or the direction of population shifts (e.g. increasing/decreasing population), so it doesn't provide those nuances.
